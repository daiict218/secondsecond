TRIBAL RIGHTS RESOURCE UNIT


Introduction

Our unit, Tribal Rights Resource Unit, works to strengthen the tribal people's Organisatoins of Southern Rajasthan, so that tribal people will get rights granted in the constitution, along with Human Rights and will live their life with dignity. Primarily, the associated POs are - Tribal Development Forum, Tribal Women's Awareness Society, Vagad Labourers' and Farmers' Association, Gordward Tribal Association and Jarga Development Society.

Tribal Rights are enshrined in the Constitution of India and have translated into several laws, but the implementation of those laws leaves a lot to desired! The Panchayats Extension to the Scheduled Areas (PESA) Act 1996, the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act 2006 are important pieces of legislation, passed by the Central and state governments, but not implemented, and so, upto now, the tribals are denied the rights intended. There are many issues on which the Tribal Rights Resource Unit will work.

The tribal people of Rajasthan are mainly of two tribes â€“ the Bhils and the Meenas. The Bhils are almost entirely excluded in taking advantage of the reservations in education and in jobs. Tribal Rights Resource Unit is working with tribal group of Bhils - Bhils, Gametis, Bhil-Meenas, Garasias. Astha will also work with other primitive tribes of Rajasthan i.e. Saharia, Kathodia.


The unit works to:

  • Establish a Tribal Rights Resource Centre which can help tribal POs to become mature and self sustainable.
  • Implement PESA - Panchayat Extension to the Scheduled Areas Act, for Tribal self rule.
  • Bring the tribal's People's Organisations on a common platform.
  • Help form and stregthen a Tribal Rights Forum of Tribal People's Organisation in Rajasthan.
  • To build the capacity of PO leaders, develop alternate leadership and take forward the process of greater maturity for grassroots leadership
  • Influence policy on the issues and problems of the tribals at the state and national level with other like-minded organisations
